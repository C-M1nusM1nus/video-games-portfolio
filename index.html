Video Game Design & AI Game Evaluation

Portfolio focused on gameplay analysis, systems critique, and AI-generated game evaluation.

This portfolio demonstrates my approach to evaluating video games through the lens of mechanics, player experience, and systemic design. The analyses emphasize identifying where games succeed, where they fall short, and how structured feedback can guide both human and AI-driven iteration.

AI-Generated Games: Evaluation & Judgment

Slipstream Runner

Momentum-Based Platformer — Mechanically Sound, Experientially Under-Tuned

Slipstream Runner succeeds in establishing a clear gameplay identity centered on speed and flow. Players immediately understand their objective, and the game introduces mechanics incrementally, reducing onboarding friction.

Movement & Game Feel

While the game emphasizes momentum, the underlying movement tuning results in a slippery, low-friction feel that limits precision. Players often feel as though they are reacting to motion rather than actively controlling it.

Mechanic Interaction

Core mechanics—jumping, dashing, and wall interactions—exist alongside one another but do not strongly reinforce each other, lowering the overall skill ceiling.

Feedback & Readability

Momentum feedback is communicated through dramatic background color shifts. While effective initially, this becomes visually distracting during extended play sessions.

Overall Assessment

Slipstream Runner illustrates a common AI-generation pattern: the game functions correctly but lacks the fine-tuning needed to achieve satisfying mastery.

Signal Lost

Indie-Scale Puzzle Game — Cohesive, Polished, and Extensible

Signal Lost presents a tightly scoped puzzle experience with consistent rules and strong feedback loops that support thoughtful problem-solving.

Core Loop & Puzzle Design

The rotation-based signal mechanic provides immediate and readable feedback, reinforcing learning without excessive trial-and-error.

Progression & Difficulty

Mechanics are introduced gradually and combined logically, ensuring difficulty increases through complexity rather than restriction.

Feedback & Polish

Sound effects and visual cues enhance clarity and player engagement without overwhelming the experience.

Overall Assessment

Signal Lost exemplifies how focused scope and thoughtful design can produce a complete, extensible indie game experience.

Game Systems Dissection & Community-Informed Evaluation

Minecraft

Minecraft’s strength lies in emergent gameplay enabled by simple mechanics. Community feedback consistently praises player freedom while noting weaknesses in combat depth and long-term structured goals.

Undertale

Undertale integrates narrative and mechanics in a way that makes player choice emotionally meaningful. Some players cite limited replayability, but its storytelling impact remains widely respected.

Ninja Gaiden (NES)

Ninja Gaiden is praised for precision and challenge, though its enemy placement and knockback mechanics are often cited as frustrating rather than instructive.

Fortnite

Fortnite’s build system creates an exceptionally high skill ceiling. Community discussion often centers on balancing accessibility with competitive depth.

AI-Specific Design Insight

Evaluating both AI-generated and human-designed games reveals that technical correctness alone does not guarantee engagement. Subtle tuning, feedback clarity, and system cohesion are critical.

This portfolio reflects the type of structured judgment required to help AI systems produce more compelling and innovative video game experiences.
